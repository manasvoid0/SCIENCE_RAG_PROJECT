Photosynthesis is the fundamental biological process by which green plants convert light energy into chemical energy. This process occurs primarily within the chloroplasts of plant cells, where the pigment chlorophyll absorbs sunlight. The absorbed energy facilitates the conversion of carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. Glucose serves as the essential energy source for the plant, while oxygen is released into the atmosphere as a byproduct. Photosynthesis is critical for sustaining life on Earth, as it forms the foundation of nearly all food chains. Furthermore, this process plays a significant role in regulating atmospheric carbon dioxide levels. In conclusion, photosynthesis is an indispensable mechanism that supports both plant growth and global ecological balance.
